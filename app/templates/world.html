The Condition of WORLD in this pandemic situation

A phase of silent aggression in human destruction!

Since 31 December 2019 and as of 29 April 2020, 3 052 370 cases of COVID-19 (in accordance with the applied case definitions and testing strategies in the affected countries) have been reported, including 216 563 deaths.

Cases have been reported from:

Africa: 34 934 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are Egypt (5 042), South Africa (4 996), Morocco (4 252), Algeria (3 649) and Cameroon (1 806).

Asia: 490 252 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are Turkey (114 653), Iran (92 584), China (83 940), India (31 332) and Saudi Arabia (20 077).

America: 1 252 601 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are United States (1 012 583), Brazil (71 886), Canada (50 015), Peru (31 190) and Ecuador (24 258).

Europe: 1 265 762 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are Spain (210 773), Italy (201 505), United Kingdom (161 145), Germany (157 641) and France (126 835).

Oceania: 8 125 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are Australia (6 738), New Zealand (1 126), Guam (145), French Polynesia (58) and Fiji (18).

Other: 696 cases have been reported from an international conveyance in Japan.

Deaths have been reported from:

Africa: 1 525 deaths; the five countries reporting most deaths are Algeria (437), Egypt (359), Morocco (165), South Africa (93) and Cameroon (59).

Asia: 17 846 deaths; the five countries reporting most deaths are Iran (5 877), China (4 637), Turkey (2 992), India (1 007) and Indonesia (773).

America: 71 085 deaths; the five countries reporting most deaths are United States (58 355), Brazil (5 017), Canada (2 859), Mexico (1 569) and Ecuador (871).

Europe: 125 986 deaths; the five countries reporting most deaths are Italy (27 359), Spain (23 822), France (23 660), United Kingdom (21 678) and Belgium (7 331).

Oceania: 114 deaths; the four countries reporting deaths are Australia (88), New Zealand (19), Guam (5) and Northern Mariana Islands (2).

Other: 7 deaths have been reported from an international conveyance in Japan.
